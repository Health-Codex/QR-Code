Educational Case Studies

Case Study: Diagnosing Pneumothorax

A 45-year-old male presented with acute shortness of breath and a history of recent trauma.

  • Clinical Presentation: Sudden onset of dyspnea, decreased breath sounds on one side.
  • POCUS Findings: Absence of lung sliding and presence of the "barcode sign" on M-mode.
  • Outcome: Timely chest tube insertion resolved symptoms and prevented further complications.

Case Study: Cardiac Tamponade

A 60-year-old female with a history of hypertension presented with hypotension and jugular venous distension.

  • Clinical Presentation: Beck's triad - hypotension, muffled heart sounds, and distended neck veins.
  • POCUS Findings: Pericardial effusion with diastolic collapse of the right atrium.
  • Outcome: Emergency pericardiocentesis was performed, stabilizing the patient.

Case Study: Abdominal Free Fluid

A 35-year-old male involved in a motor vehicle accident presented with flank pain and hypotension.

  • Clinical Presentation: Hemodynamic instability and abdominal tenderness.
  • POCUS Findings: Free fluid in the hepatorenal pouch (Morison's pouch).
  • Outcome: Emergency laparotomy revealed splenic rupture, which was repaired surgically.

Case Study: Pleural Effusion

A 55-year-old patient with a history of lung cancer presented with progressive dyspnea.

  • Clinical Presentation: Dullness to percussion and decreased breath sounds over the lower lung zones.
  • POCUS Findings: Anechoic fluid collection in the pleural space with lung compression.
  • Outcome: Thoracentesis was safely performed, significantly improving respiratory function.
