Interactive Clinical Case Studies

Case Study: Acute Shortness of Breath Post-Trauma

A 45-year-old male presented with acute shortness of breath after a fall.

Clinical Presentation:
Sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, tachypnea, hypoxia, decreased breath sounds on right.
POCUS Application:
Lung Ultrasound.
Key Question:
Is there evidence of pneumothorax?

Case Study: Hypotension & Distended Neck Veins

A 60-year-old female with a history of malignancy presented with shock.

Clinical Presentation:
Hypotension, tachycardia, JVD, muffled heart sounds (Beck's triad).
POCUS Application:
Cardiac Ultrasound (Focused Echo).
Key Question:
Is there pericardial effusion causing tamponade?

Case Study: Blunt Abdominal Trauma & Instability

A 35-year-old male involved in an MVC presented with hypotension.

Clinical Presentation:
Hypotension, tachycardia, abdominal tenderness after high-speed MVC.
POCUS Application:
FAST Exam (Focused Assessment with Sonography for Trauma).
Key Question:
Is there evidence of intra-abdominal free fluid (hemoperitoneum)?

Case Study: Progressive Dyspnea in Cancer Patient

A 55-year-old patient with known lung cancer presented with worsening shortness of breath.

Clinical Presentation:
Worsening dyspnea, orthopnea, dullness to percussion, decreased breath sounds left base.
POCUS Application:
Lung Ultrasound.
Key Question:
Is there a pleural effusion, and can it be safely drained?

Case Study: Unilateral Leg Swelling

A 68-year-old male presented with acute swelling and pain in his left leg.

Clinical Presentation:
Left calf swelling, erythema, tenderness.
POCUS Application:
Lower Extremity Venous Ultrasound (2-Point Compression).
Key Question:
Is there evidence of DVT in the common femoral or popliteal veins?

Case Study: Right Upper Quadrant Pain

A 42-year-old female presented with severe RUQ pain, nausea, and fever.

Clinical Presentation:
RUQ pain radiating to shoulder, nausea, vomiting, fever, positive Murphy's sign.
POCUS Application:
RUQ / Gallbladder Ultrasound.
Key Question:
Are there signs of acute cholecystitis?

Case Study: Elderly Male with Back Pain & Hypotension

A 75-year-old male with hypertension presented with sudden onset back pain and syncope.

Clinical Presentation:
Sudden severe back/abdominal pain, syncope, hypotension, pulsatile abdominal mass (sometimes).
POCUS Application:
Abdominal Aorta Ultrasound.
Key Question:
Is there an abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA)?
